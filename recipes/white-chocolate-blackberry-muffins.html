White Chocolate Blackberry Muffins

Description

Delicious and easy muffins. Crunchy top, gooey berries and rich white chocolate throughout. Who could resist?

Makes about 14 muffins

Ingredients

  • 2 ⅓ cups all purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp ground roasted flaxseed; this can be substituted by 1tbsp almond/hazelnut oil, or left out entirely
  • ½ cup unsalted butter melted and cooled
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup Greek yoghurt, room temperature
  • 2 tbsp milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 180 g white chocolate buttons
  • 1 ½ cups fresh blackberries
  • 14 muffins cases

Other materials

  • 2 muffin trays
  • 14 muffin cases
  • mixing bowl
  • whisk or baking spatula

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 175C.
  2. Line 2 muffin trays with muffin cases. Set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, sift 2 ⅓ cups flour, 1 cup sugar, 1 tsp baking powder, ½ tsp cinnamon and ½ tsp salt. Whisk in 2 tbsp flaxseed until combined. Set aside.
  4. In another bowl, ideally with an electric hand mixer or whisk, beat ½ cup melted butter, 2 eggs, 1 cup yoghurt, 2 tbsp milk and 1 tsp vanilla on medium speed until smooth and incorporated (2-3 minutes)
  5. Fold your wet ingredients into your dry ingredients just until combined. Add white chocolate and blackberries and stir just until evenly distributed. Do not over mix. The batter will be thick and lumpy.
  6. Scoop the batter into your muffin liners (at least ¾ full), making sure you get a piece of fruit in every scoop.
  7. Bake 30-35 minutes or until golden brown and the muffins bounce back when gently pressed. Cool in the pan for 5-10 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.
